Implications

Where do we go from here?

Only 1% of women-led startups and 0.7% of ethnic minority founders received VC funding in Europe in 2021 1. This can make one feel that the state of the European startup ecosystem is a lost cause, but there is power in numbers, and hope when we come together to support the creation of equity for all founders 2, 3. Whether you’re a founder, investor, policymaker, ally, or simply interested in the topic, we’ve collected further resources and tips below to help you in creating a more equitable startup ecosystem. Take a look below.

For Founders

  1. Find your community
    1. Join mission-aligned organisations (full list here)
    2. Participate in events: pitch competitions, networking, and office hours with VCs (full list here)
  2. Practice tactics to address and respond to prejudice
    1. Find tips here
  3. Don’t be afraid to promote yourself
    1. Did you know that self-promotion has a plethora of benefits ranging from taking control of how you’re perceived to opening doors to new opportunities? (Full list here) #IamRemarkable workshop
  4. Further Resources:
    1. How to Raise Startup Funds as a Woman of Color
    2. Fundraising Opportunities for minorities and Women of Color

For Investors

  1. Implement (un)conscious bias training
  2. Listen to underrepresented voices in your organisation and community
  3. Hire diverse talent for all levels of your firm
  4. Get involved
    1. Offer pro-bono mentoring
    2. Join Beacon: Founderland’s Investor Collective
  5. Further Resources:
    1. The Power of Diversity: Why homogenous teams in venture capital are bad for business
    2. A VC’s Guide to Investing in Black Founders
    3. List of anti-racist and DEI leaders that we recommend

For Policymakers

  1. Collect basic demographic data on ethnicity and gender 4 to get a clearer picture of the systemic challenges underserved people face. We cannot create change without seeing the problem.
  2. Practice tactics to address and respond to prejudice
    1. Find tips here
  3. Invest in long-term educational and social equity projects and policies 5
  4. Create a visa that makes it easier for people with migration backgrounds to start a company in Germany. Every fifth startup in Germany is founded by a person with a migration background; however, they frequently face obstacles due to their migration status. A successful example of this is the French Tech Visa for startup employees, founders, and investors 6
